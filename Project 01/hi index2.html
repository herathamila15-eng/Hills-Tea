Ceylon Hand Made Black Tea


What Is Ceylon Black Tea?

Ceylon tea refers to tea that comes from Sri Lanka (formerly Ceylon). It’s one of the most famous black teas in the world, known for its rich flavour, bright colour, and distinctive aroma. The term “Ceylon” is protected — that means only tea grown and processed in Sri Lanka can be legally called Ceylon tea.

What Does “Handmade Black Tea” Mean?

“Handmade” means the tea leaves are handled and processed with human skill rather than fully machine-automated methods. Hand-making typically involves:

  1. Hand-picking: Workers carefully pick only the tender top leaves and buds — this helps ensure better flavour and quality.
  2. Withering: The leaves lose moisture and become soft.
  3. Rolling: Leaves are rolled by hand or in small batches to break cells, starting oxidation (what turns green leaves to brown).
  4. Oxidation: Leaves are left to oxidise fully — this develops the classic robust taste and dark colour of black tea.
  5. Drying & Sorting: The tea is dried and sorted into grades — often whole leaves (like Orange Pekoe) or smaller broken pieces.
